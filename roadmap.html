Roadmap

Where VoxeraOS is headed

Stability before scale. Operator trust before expanding capabilities. VoxeraOS is an open-source alpha — a one-person evenings/weekends project. Here's what's built and the broad direction for where things are going. Details will evolve.

Current release

v0.1.8 — Open-source alpha

Built

Governed queue execution

Durable filesystem-backed queue with explicit job lifecycle, approval gates that pause execution, and artifact-backed outcomes for every job.

Built

Operator visibility and recovery

Operator panel with health-at-a-glance, hygiene tools, baseline recovery directories, quarantine buckets, and incident bundles.

Built

Vera v1 — conversational surface

Clear Vera / VoxeraOS separation, deterministic prompt ordering, brain fallback classification, and the most stable Vera so far.

Broad direction

Where things are headed

These are directional themes, not detailed delivery commitments. Specifics will be shaped by what the project learns along the way. This is a one-person evenings/weekends project — pace and priorities will shift.

Next themes

Hardening and resilience

Richer recovery inspection, degradation-aware runtime, intelligent backoff, and broader operator tooling.

On the horizon

Voice-first and AI-first

Voice loop, signed skills, platform expansion, and packaging improvements. Long-term direction — many things will change.

Our philosophy

Grounded in what AI is missing today

This project is grounded in present-day gaps in AI systems: trust, review, execution boundaries, and proof of outcome. The alpha establishes the control structure — governed execution, approval gating, audit records, and safe defaults — before expanding capabilities. VoxeraOS is open source now.
